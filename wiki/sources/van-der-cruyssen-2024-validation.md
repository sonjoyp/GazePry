Van der Cruyssen et al. — *The validation of online webcam-based eye-tracking:
The replication of the cascade effect, the novelty preference, and the visual
world paradigm*, **Behavior Research Methods 2024**. Replicates three classic
eye-tracking effects with **WebGazer.js** — part of the
[[webcam-tracking-validation]] argument. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year.*
(`raw/The validation of online webcam-based eye-tracking...-2024.pdf`)

## Key facts

- All three effects (cascade n=134, novelty preference n=45, visual world
  paradigm n=32) replicated online, but effect sizes **shrank 20–27%**; a
  same-participant lab-vs-online comparison confirmed the shrinkage is partly
  webcam noise, not just replication.
- Practical bound: webcam gaze works for effects that don't need high precision
  — roughly **≤4 large regions of interest**. Directly bounds what
  content-*dependent* AOI reading (D2) can expect on a webcam.
