Bozkir, Bühler, Wu, Kasneci, Bauer, Cranor — *The impact of device type, data
practices, and use case scenarios on privacy concerns about eye-tracked
augmented reality in the United States and Germany*, **Journal of Cybersecurity
2025** — bibliography **[28]**. A user-attitudes study — background for the
consent design and the SOUPS-companion angle. (`raw/The impact of device
type...-2025.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Cross-national (US / Germany) study of how **device type, data practices, and
  use-case scenario** shape privacy concern about eye-tracked AR.
- Supplies the attitudes evidence for GazePry's consent-design discussion —
  what users are (and are not) concerned about, informing the
  [[enabling-conditions|camera-consent gap]] framing and a possible SOUPS
  consent study.
