Huang, White & Dumais — *No Clicks, No Problem: Using Cursor Movements to
Understand and Improve Search*, **CHI '11**, pp. 1225–1234,
doi:10.1145/1978942.1979125. Shows **mouse cursor behavior on SERPs** (movements,
hovers — not just clicks) reveals result examination **at Web scale, where gaze
tracking is impractical**. The single strongest support for the permission-free
[[cursor-tracking]] floor — and the paper the [[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2
direction]] inverts into *"No Clicks, No Privacy."* Cited **author-year** (not in
plan §21). *(Grounding: retrieved abstract/metadata, web search 2026-07-16 — not a
full-PDF read.)*

## Key facts

- Research questions: (i) how far gaze correlates with cursor on SERPs vs
  non-SERPs; (ii) what cursor behavior reveals about **result-examination
  strategies** and how it relates to clicks and prior eye-tracking; (iii) whether
  **large-scale** cursor data enables useful applications.
- Core claim for D2: examination that leaves **no click** is recoverable from the
  cursor — exactly the *considered-but-unclicked* / zero-click signal the attack
  targets.
- The "at scale, no eye tracker needed" framing is what makes the cursor channel a
  **silent, permission-free** surveillance surface once reframed adversarially.
