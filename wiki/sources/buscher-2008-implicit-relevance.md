Buscher, Dengel & van Elst — *Eye Movements as Implicit Relevance Feedback*,
**CHI '08 Extended Abstracts**, pp. 2991–2996, doi:10.1145/1358628.1358796.
Recovers, from **reading behavior**, which content a user found relevant — the
sub-document signal the [[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2 direction]] reframes as
*leakage of what within the content mattered to you*. Cited **author-year** (not in
plan §21). *(Grounding: retrieved abstract/metadata, web search 2026-07-16 — not a
full-PDF read.)*

## Key facts

- Finding: users **skim irrelevant** documents but exert **continuous reading**
  behavior on relevant ones — so gaze discriminates read-vs-skimmed-vs-relevant at
  the passage level.
- Framed as **implicit relevance feedback** for IR personalization; the D2
  reframing is the adversarial inversion (recover it *without the user's
  knowledge*).
- Companions (retrieve if needed): a SIGIR '08 query-expansion paper (gaze-based
  feedback at the subdocument level) and the fuller
  [[buscher-2012-attentive-documents|Attentive Documents]] (ACM TiiS 2012).
