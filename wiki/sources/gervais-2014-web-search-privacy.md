Gervais, Shokri, Singla, Capkun & Lenders — *Quantifying Web-Search Privacy*,
**ACM CCS '14**, doi:10.1145/2660267.2660367. A framework to **measure** the
privacy loss a user suffers from search-query-log observation — the top-security-
venue precedent that web-search privacy belongs at CCS/USENIX/S&P, cited by the
[[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2 direction]] to justify its venue targeting.
Cited **author-year** (not in plan §21). *(Grounding: retrieved abstract/metadata,
web search 2026-07-16 — not a full-PDF read; verify the author list against the
published version.)*

## Key facts

- Quantifies web-search privacy against an adversary observing **query logs** —
  again the queries+clicks layer, not examination.
- For D2: (a) methodological precedent for a **privacy-quantification** framing
  (the surveillance-surplus metric is D2's analogue); (b) evidence that search
  privacy is a top-venue topic (Gervais et al. is CCS 2014, same proceedings as
  Acar's *The Web Never Forgets* [44]).
