Kaduk, Goeke, Finger, König — *Webcam eye tracking close to laboratory
standards: Comparing a new webcam-based system and the EyeLink 1000*,
**Behavior Research Methods 2024**. The strongest single data point in the
[[webcam-tracking-validation]] argument: a webcam system recorded
**simultaneously** against a gold-standard IR tracker. *Not in plan §21 — cite
author-year.* (`raw/Webcam eye tracking close to laboratory standards...-2024.pdf`)

## Key facts

- System: Labvanced's webcam eye tracker vs **EyeLink 1000** (SR Research),
  same subjects, same trials, five-task standardized battery (Large Grid,
  Smooth Pursuit, natural images, two Head-Movement tasks).
- **Accuracy 1.4°, precision 1.1°** — only ≈0.5° worse than EyeLink; slightly
  better centrally (1.3° / 0.9°). Raw-gaze-sample correlation ≈**90%** (Large
  Grid), ≈80% (Free View / Smooth Pursuit).
- Puts webcam gaze "roughly on par with mobile eye-tracking devices" and a
  "substantial improvement" over the WebGazer/SearchGazer generation — the
  clearest evidence the accuracy objection is closing.
