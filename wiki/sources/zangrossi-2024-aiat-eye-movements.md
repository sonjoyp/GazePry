Zangrossi, Gatto, Lanfranchi, Scarpazza, Celli & Sartori — *Autobiographical
implicit association test and eye movements: fixations topography enables
detection of autobiographical memories*, **Frontiers in Psychology 15, art.
1268256, 2024**, doi 10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1268256. Evidence that gaze-based memory
detection **survives a one-week delay** — the key durability data point for
[[recognition-knowledge-leakage]]. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year (doc-local
[C5] in [[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- Mock-crime paradigm: guilty (n = 33) destroyed a photograph, innocent (n = 35)
  wrote a code on paper. **All tested one week later** with an autobiographical
  IAT plus eye tracking.
- Measure: **fixation topography** — proportion and count of fixations to category
  label boxes (AOIs) across compatible/incompatible blocks; formalized as an
  **"eye-D index"** analogous to the aIAT-D but over fixation distributions
  instead of reaction times.
- **Accuracy: aIAT-D alone 86.7%** (sensitivity 78.8%, specificity 94.3%);
  **eye-D index alone 75%**; combined, sensitivity rose to 85%.
- Two things D7 takes from this: (a) a **coarse, box-level AOI** measure is
  sufficient — no within-image AOIs needed; (b) the memory signal is still
  recoverable **a week after encoding**, supporting D7's E1 delay condition.
