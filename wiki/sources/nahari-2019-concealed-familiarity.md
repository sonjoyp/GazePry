Nahari, Lancry-Dayan, Ben-Shakhar & Pertzov — *Detecting concealed familiarity
using eye movements: the role of task demands*, **Cognitive Research: Principles
and Implications 4, art. 10, 2019**, doi 10.1186/s41235-019-0162-7. Establishes
that **the wrapping task decides whether familiarity-driven gaze can be
voluntarily suppressed** — the direct source of D7's cover-task manipulation.
*Not in plan §21 — cite author-year (doc-local [C2] in
[[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- **n = 61** (33 concealed experiment, 28 countermeasures experiment).
- **Stimulus layout: four faces presented simultaneously in parallel displays**,
  followed by single-face presentations. Four coarse, well-separated regions is
  within the webcam bound established by [[van-der-cruyssen-2024-validation]] —
  which is why D7 caps its arrays at 4 tiles.
- Measures: dwell time, number of visits and visit duration, number of fixations
  and fixation duration, mean fixation duration on single displays.
- **The headline finding:** in a *visual detection* task, countermeasure
  instructions successfully concealed familiarity; in a *short-term memory* task,
  "the strong avoidance was evident even when participants were explicitly
  instructed to conceal their familiarity." Conclusion: **tasks that encourage
  differential gaze behaviour are more resistant to countermeasures.**
- Note the **direction of effect is avoidance**, not preference, in this
  paradigm — one of the reasons D7 pre-registers per-window directional
  predictions rather than assuming a sign.
