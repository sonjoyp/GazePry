Kröger, Lutz, Müller (TU Berlin / Fraunhofer / Weizenbaum) — *What Does Your
Gaze Reveal About You? On the Privacy Implications of Eye Tracking*, **Springer
2020** — bibliography **[21]**. The structured catalog of *what personal data
is inferable from eye activity* — the backbone of GazePry's "three classes of
leaked information" and the D5 attribute vector. (`raw/What Does Your Gaze
Reveal About You...-2020.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Reviews, across disciplines, personal data implicitly inferable from recorded
  eye activity: **biometric identity, gender, age, ethnicity, body weight,
  personality traits, drug use, emotional/cognitive state, and more**.
- The reference the plan/report use for the third leakage class — **stable
  personal attributes** (D5) — and for the general "gaze reveals more than the
  user intends" premise.
- Argues eye tracking is becoming cheap/compact/pervasive (gaming, marketing,
  driver safety, healthcare) — the proliferation that motivates the whole
  project.
