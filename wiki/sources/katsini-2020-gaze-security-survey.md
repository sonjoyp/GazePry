Katsini, Abdrabou, Raptis, Khamis, Alt — *The Role of Eye Gaze in Security and
Privacy Applications: Survey and Future HCI Research Directions*, **CHI 2020** —
bibliography **[3]**. The survey GazePry cites for "these risk categories are
well-established, not hypothetical," and a source for the SOUPS-companion
consent/HCI angle. (`raw/The Role of Eye Gaze in Security and Privacy
Applications...-2020.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Structured survey of how eye gaze is used in **security & privacy**
  (authentication, gaze-based passwords, attention/attack detection) and the
  privacy risks it creates; lays out future HCI research directions.
- One of the two survey anchors (with [[kroger-2020-gaze-privacy|Kröger et al.]]
  [21]) the plan uses to establish that gaze-leakage categories are treated as
  established risks in the literature.
