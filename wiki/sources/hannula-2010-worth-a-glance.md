Hannula, Althoff, Warren, Riggs, Cohen & Ryan — *Worth a Glance: Using Eye
Movements to Investigate the Cognitive Neuroscience of Memory*, **Frontiers in
Human Neuroscience 4, art. 166, 2010**, doi 10.3389/fnhum.2010.00166. The review
that generalizes the [[eye-movement-memory-effect]] beyond faces and supplies
the claim D7 leans on hardest: the effect **does not require conscious
recollection**. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year (doc-local [M2] in
[[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- Eye movements reveal memory for elements of previous experience; the effects
  **emerge rapidly** and change visual processing efficiency.
- Critically for [[recognition-knowledge-leakage]]: the effects occur **without
  requiring conscious recollection** — memory is expressed in gaze whether or not
  the viewer reports (or notices) recognizing anything.
- Provided evidence on the hippocampus's role in **relational** memory, distinct
  from explicit memory or conscious recollection.
