Schwedes & Wentura — *The revealing glance: Eye gaze behavior to concealed
information*, **Memory & Cognition, 2012**, doi 10.3758/s13421-011-0173-1. The
cleanest "**intent does not gate the signal**" result in the
[[ocular-concealed-information-test]] literature, and the source of D7's
fixation-duration feature family. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year (doc-local
[C1] in [[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- **Six-face arrays.** Three conditions: a known face among six that must be
  *concealed* (participant selects a different face); a known face that must be
  *revealed*; or a neutral all-unknown display.
- **Fixation duration revealed memory regardless of the subsequent intention** to
  reveal or conceal — longer fixations on known faces even when recognition was
  being actively concealed.
- **Concealed knowledge detected in 65% of relevant trials.**
- Builds on Ryan, Hannula & Cohen (2007): fixations on a familiar face are longer
  than on an unknown face.
