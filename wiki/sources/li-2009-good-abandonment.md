Li, Huffman & Tokuda — *Good Abandonment in Mobile and PC Internet Search*,
**SIGIR '09**, pp. 43–50, doi:10.1145/1571941.1571951. Defines **good
abandonment** — a query whose information need is met **on the SERP, with no
click** — the *zero-click* behavior whose intent the
[[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2 direction]] recovers (RQ2), and which the
clickstream by construction
cannot contain. Cited **author-year** (not in plan §21). *(Grounding: retrieved
abstract/metadata, web search 2026-07-16 — not a full-PDF read.)*

## Key facts

- Coined/analyzed **good abandonment** across PC and mobile in three locales;
  motivates that satisfied intent increasingly leaves **no click trail**.
- For D2: the click baseline recovers *none* of this intent, so gaze/cursor
  examination recovery here is pure **surveillance surplus**.
