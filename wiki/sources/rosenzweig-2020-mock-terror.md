Rosenzweig & Bonneh — *Concealed information revealed by involuntary eye
movements on the fringe of awareness in a mock terror experiment*, **Scientific
Reports 10, art. 14355, 2020**, doi 10.1038/s41598-020-71487-9. The
[[ocular-concealed-information-test]]'s **high-water mark** — and, for GazePry, a
**ceiling that the commodity webcam channel cannot reach**. *Not in plan §21 —
cite author-year (doc-local [C4] in [[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- **n = 25** (13 study group, 12 controls). Single-item sequential (RSVP-style)
  presentation at ~1 Hz: target flashed **10 ms**, blank 60 ms, then two
  successive images at 50 ms each.
- Measures are **microsaccade rate modulation functions and microsaccade reaction
  time** (latency of the first microsaccade in a 200–1000 ms window), *not* dwell
  time or fixation counts.
- Results: **100% correct probe identification** and 95% discrimination of
  "terrorists" from innocents when the target was known; **88% classification,
  AUC 0.84** in the target-unknown case.
- **Why D7 cites it as a ceiling only:** microsaccade timing at 10 ms stimulus
  resolution requires 500–1000 Hz sampling. A commodity webcam runs at a ~30 Hz
  true frame rate ([[webcam-tracking-validation]]). This design is
  **categorically unavailable** in the browser, and D7 §9.2 says so explicitly to
  pre-empt a reviewer asking why webcam numbers are lower.
- Limitation the authors acknowledge: **no deception manipulation** — the study
  group was not instructed to conceal.
