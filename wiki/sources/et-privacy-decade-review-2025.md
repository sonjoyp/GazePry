## Key facts

*(All provisional, from search snippets.)*

- Describes itself as the first data-driven review of eye-tracking privacy
  research, covering **gaze, iris, and eye-image data** across immersive, mobile,
  and clinical contexts.
- **78 papers, 2015–2025**, analyzed with ensemble topic modelling (non-negative
  matrix factorization); **nine topics** emerged.
- Reported split: **normative** work emphasizes *inference of identity and
  personal traits* as the unresolved risk, while **technical** work treats
  privacy as a computational problem and mitigates within specific contexts.
- If that split holds on a full read, it is direct support for the D7 gap: the
  field's normative attention is on **identity and traits**
  ([[gaze-re-identification]], attribute inference), not on **memory contents**.
