Semmelmann & Weigelt — *Online webcam-based eye tracking in cognitive science:
A first look*, **Behavior Research Methods 2018**. An early validation of
[[webgazer]] for online cognitive-science experiments — one data point in the
[[webcam-tracking-validation]] argument. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year.*
(`raw/Online webcam-based eye tracking...-2018.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Three tasks (fixation, pursuit, free-viewing), online vs in-lab. Fixation
  offset ≈**172 px in-lab / ≈207 px online** (≈15% vs 18% of screen; ≈3.94°
  visual angle); pursuit ≈211 px in-lab / 216 px online; higher variance
  online.
- Free-viewing replicated the known face-attention pattern (eyes 28.25% vs
  nose 9.71%, mouth 4.00%).
- Verdict: webcam eye tracking is "suitable for all three tasks" but noisier —
  the honest early baseline before head-pose-aware methods.
