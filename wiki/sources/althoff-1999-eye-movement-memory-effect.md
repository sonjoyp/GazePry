Althoff & Cohen — *Eye-Movement-Based Memory Effect: A Reprocessing Effect in
Face Perception*, **Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, and
Cognition 25(4), 997–1010, 1999**. The foundational demonstration of the
[[eye-movement-memory-effect]]: previously seen faces are *sampled differently*
from novel ones. This is the mechanism [[recognition-knowledge-leakage]] (D7)
weaponizes. *Not in plan §21 — cite author-year (doc-local label [M1] in
[[d7-recognition-knowledge-direction]]).*

## Key facts

- Two experiments contrasting **famous vs non-famous faces** across a range of
  processing tasks; different eye-movement patterns elicited by prior exposure.
- **The effect emerges within the first five fixations** — the single most
  important fact for D7 feasibility, because it means the attack needs a *short*
  observation window, not a long one.
- Observed on **multiple** eye-movement measures, reflecting a change in the
  viewer's sampling behaviour rather than a single narrow index.
- Authors argue the effect is an **obligatory consequence of previous exposure**
  — a reprocessing effect from re-engaging visual pattern analyzers. "Obligatory"
  is what makes it a *leak* rather than a disclosure.
