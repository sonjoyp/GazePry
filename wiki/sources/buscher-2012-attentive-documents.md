Buscher, Dengel, Biedert & van Elst — *Attentive Documents: Eye Tracking as
Implicit Feedback for Information Retrieval and Beyond*, **ACM Trans. Interactive
Intelligent Systems (TiiS)**, vol. 1, no. 2, art. 9, 2012,
doi:10.1145/2070719.2070722. The extended treatment of recovering **sub-document
relevance from reading behavior** — the finer-grained reading-recovery signal
behind the [[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2 direction]]'s reading surface and its
RQ5 utility metric. Cited **author-year** (not in plan §21). *(Grounding: retrieved
abstract/metadata, web search 2026-07-16 — not a full-PDF read.)*

## Key facts

- Journal extension of [[buscher-2008-implicit-relevance]]: gaze → implicit
  feedback at the **paragraph / subdocument** level, for IR and "beyond."
- For D2: (a) evidence that *which passages mattered* is recoverable from gaze;
  (b) the natural **utility task** for the RQ5 defense (relevance-feedback quality
  that a countermeasure must preserve).
