Miller, Herrera, Jun, Landay, Bailenson (Stanford) — *Personal identifiability
of user tracking data during observation of 360-degree VR video*, **Scientific
Reports 2020** — bibliography **[40]**. Identification from *ordinary* VR
viewing with **no identifying task designed** — the closest VR analogue to
GazePry's RQ4 unclearability claim. (`raw/Personal identifiability...-2020.pdf`)

## Key facts

- **511 participants**, 360° VR video viewing; system identifies **95%** of
  users correctly from **< 5 min** of head/hand tracking per person.
- The key methodological parallel: identification works on **naturalistic,
  non-designed** behavior — exactly GazePry's content-independent, no-task
  premise (the task is whatever site the visitor is on).
- The plan (§18.4) frames this as a "re-identifiable **across device types**"
  analogue for the RQ4 cross-device / [[unclearability]] claim; the paper's
  headline is the 95%/511/<5 min naturalistic result.
