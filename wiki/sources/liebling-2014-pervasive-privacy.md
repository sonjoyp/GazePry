Liebling & Preibusch — *Privacy Considerations for a Pervasive Eye Tracking
World*, **UbiComp 2014 (Adjunct)** — bibliography **[6]**. An early position
paper arguing gaze data needs policy + data-hygiene protection — and, crucially
for GazePry, that **gaze tracking is more opaque to users than webcams**.
(`raw/Privacy considerations for a pervasive eye tracking world...-2014.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Predicts gaze trackers will "follow the path of webcams" — cheap, ubiquitous —
  and carry the same privacy concerns, but with a twist: webcam privacy loss is
  *obvious* to users, whereas **gaze tracking is opaque** and deserves special
  attention.
- That opacity is exactly the [[enabling-conditions|camera-consent gap]]
  GazePry relies on: a user who consents to a camera does not perceive that
  gaze *dynamics* are being extracted and used for identity.
- Argues for protection by both **policy and good data hygiene** — the
  consent/policy framing for a SOUPS companion.
