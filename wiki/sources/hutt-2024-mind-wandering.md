Hutt, Wong, Papoutsaki, Baker, Gold, Mills — *Webcam-based eye tracking to
detect mind wandering and comprehension errors*, **Behavior Research Methods
2024** — bibliography **[22]**. The evidence for the D3 cognitive/affective
vector on a *commodity webcam*: [[webgazer]]-grade gaze detects covert
cognitive states above chance. (`raw/Webcam-based eye tracking to detect mind
wandering...-2024.pdf`)

## Key facts

- Used **WebGazer** during an online reading-comprehension task to detect
  **task-unrelated thought (mind wandering)** and **comprehension**, from a
  single calibration, across two populations: university students (N=105) and a
  diverse Prolific sample (N=173, <20% White).
- Gaze measurements were "sufficiently accurate and precise" to predict both
  states; predicted mind-wandering rate correlated with comprehension scores
  (predictive validity). Cross-dataset generalization tested (train Study 1 →
  test Study 2 and vice versa).
- Slicing analyses over lighting, glasses, etc. — the same real-world-variation
  conditions the plan's [[conditions-matrix]] includes.
- Anchors the claim (report §3, plan §3.2) that content-*independent* dynamics
  leak **cognitive/affective state**, not just identity — vector D3 of
  [[leakage-vectors-d1-d6]], on the webcam channel specifically.
