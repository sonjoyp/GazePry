Lohr, Aziz, Friedman, Komogortsev (Texas State) — *GazeBaseVR, a large-scale,
longitudinal, binocular eye-tracking dataset collected in virtual reality*,
**Scientific Data 2023** — bibliography **[37]**. The binocular VR extension of
[[griffith-2021-gazebase|GazeBase]]; useful for the gallery-size scaling curve
and a VR contrast point. (`raw/GazeBaseVR...-2023.pdf`)

## Key facts

- **407 college-aged participants**, **5,020 binocular recordings**, ET-enabled
  VR headset **@ 250 Hz**, **up to six sessions over 26 months**. Five tasks:
  vergence, horizontal smooth pursuit, video-viewing, self-paced reading,
  random oblique saccade.
- Many participants overlap with GazeBase and other datasets (cross-device
  linkage potential); 11 recorded before/after COVID infection.
- The 400+-user VR video-watching population [[patergianakis-2026-xr-anonymity]]
  reports ≈96.6% identification on.
- **DOI:** **10.1038/s41597-023-02075-5** (confirmed from the PDF) — the plan
  §21 [37] value "…02073-7" is a **typo**; `raw/related-papers.txt` had it
  right.
