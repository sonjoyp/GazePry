Rayner — *Eye Movements in Reading and Information Processing: 20 Years of
Research*, **Psychological Bulletin**, vol. 124, no. 3, pp. 372–422, 1998,
doi:10.1037/0033-2909.124.3.372. The canonical review of **reading eye
movements** — fixations, saccades, perceptual span, **regressions**, and
individual differences — the psychological foundation for the reading surface of
the [[reading-search-intent-leakage|D2 direction]] and the source of the *re-read
= difficulty/importance* interpretation. Cited **author-year** (not in plan §21).
*(Grounding: retrieved abstract/metadata, web search 2026-07-16 — not a full-PDF
read.)*

## Key facts

- Establishes that eye-movement data reflect **moment-to-moment cognitive
  processing** in reading and related tasks.
- Names the constructs D2's reading features use: fixation duration, saccade
  amplitude, **regressions (re-reading)**, perceptual span, individual
  differences.
- For RQ0: also the source of the **population reading prior** (top-to-bottom,
  F-pattern) that the D2 attack must beat, not merely reproduce.
